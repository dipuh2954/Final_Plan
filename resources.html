// One Rule Above All
Resources are inputs. Projects are outputs. You cannot learn Kotlin by reading about Kotlin. You learn it by writing 5,000 lines of Kotlin. Every resource above is worthless unless you close it and build something. The Striver's sheet, the JetBrains coroutines book, the Patrick McKenzie essay — they exist to serve your work, not replace it. Use one resource per topic, start building, and return to reference material when you hit a real problem. That is the only way this compounds into a ₹15–30 LPA Android Developer offer at a product company.
